About Harvard FCU

Harvard Federal Credit Union was founded with a simple mission: to provide accessible, affordable financial services to the Boston community. For over three decades, we've remained committed to putting Boston members first, offering products and services that traditional banks simply cannot match. Our journey began when a group of dedicated Boston professionals recognized the need for a financial institution that truly cared about its members' wellbeing. Today, we serve thousands of Boston members across multiple states, maintaining the same values of integrity, transparency, and member-centricity that defined our founding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Harvard FCU membership and services

Who is eligible to join Harvard FCU?

Harvard FCU membership is available to individuals who meet our field of membership criteria. This typically includes employees of Harvard University and affiliated institutions, their families, and members of certain professional organizations. We also offer membership to those who live, work, or attend school in our service areas. To determine your eligibility, we recommend contacting our membership team directly at website@harvard-fcu.org or visiting our website at harvard-fcu.org. Our representatives will be happy to guide you through the membership process and answer any specific questions about your eligibility.

What makes Harvard FCU different from traditional banks?

As a member-owned credit union, Harvard FCU operates under a fundamentally different business model than traditional banks. We're a not-for-profit institution focused on serving our members' best interests rather than maximizing shareholder profits. This means we can offer competitive interest rates, lower fees, and personalized service. Our members have a voice in how we operate through our democratic governance structure. Additionally, we invest in community development and member education, providing free financial counseling and resources to help you achieve your financial goals. Our commitment to transparency means you'll never encounter hidden fees or surprise charges.

How is my money protected at Harvard FCU?

Your deposits at Harvard FCU are protected by the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA), which is the federal agency that insures credit union deposits. Each member's deposits are insured up to $250,000 per account category, which covers checking accounts, savings accounts, money market accounts, and individual retirement accounts separately. This means your money is as safe with us as it would be at any bank insured by the FDIC. Additionally, we employ advanced security measures including encryption technology, secure data centers, and regular security audits to protect your personal and financial information from fraud and unauthorized access.

What loan products does Harvard FCU offer?

Harvard FCU offers a comprehensive range of loan products to meet various financial needs. Our offerings include auto loans with competitive rates, home mortgages with flexible terms, personal loans for consolidation or major purchases, home equity lines of credit, and business loans for entrepreneurs and small business owners. We also offer student loan refinancing options to help borrowers reduce their interest rates and monthly payments. Each loan product is designed with flexibility in mind, and our expert loan officers work with you to find terms that fit your budget and timeline. We encourage you to speak with one of our advisors to discuss which loan product best suits your needs.

How can I access my account and manage my finances?

Harvard FCU provides multiple convenient ways to access and manage your accounts. Our online banking platform allows you to check balances, transfer funds, pay bills, and apply for loans from any computer with internet access. We also offer a mobile banking app for iOS and Android devices, enabling you to manage your finances on the go. Additionally, our ATM network provides access to your cash whenever you need it, with no surcharge fees at our network locations. For those who prefer personal service, our member service centers are staffed with knowledgeable professionals ready to assist you. We also offer telephone banking for account inquiries and transactions during business hours.
